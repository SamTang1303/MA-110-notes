# Theorem 8.9 MA-110

> [!definition] Theorem 8.9
> Let $F$ be a field, and let $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ be nonzero polynomials in $F[x]$. Then there exists polynomials $a(x)$ and $b(x)$ in $F[x]$ such that
> $$
> d(x) = a(x)f(x)+b(x)g(x)
> $$
> is the greatest common divisor of $f(x)$ and $g(x)$.
